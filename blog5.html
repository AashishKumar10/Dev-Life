Blinkit Clone – UI & Branding
Experiment

During my frontend learning journey, I decided to recreate the Blinkit (formerly Grofers) homepage to better understand the importance of UI consistency and brand-focused design.

The idea was simple — take inspiration from Blinkit’s clean, green-yellow themed layout and try to build a static clone using only HTML and CSS. I focused on designing the header section, search bar, categories, product cards, and the cart icon area. This project gave me practical experience in arranging grid layouts and maintaining visual hierarchy.

One of the most exciting parts was selecting the right shades of green and yellow that match Blinkit's brand identity. I also worked on a promotional banner (with a cart icon) and practiced using icons, hover effects, and call-to-action buttons.

Though the site doesn’t have backend functionality, this clone taught me how to balance colors, whitespace, typography, and responsive behavior for different screen sizes. It improved my confidence in UI/UX thinking, which is very useful while working on real e-commerce websites.

This blog covers my full thought process — from layout structuring to pixel-perfect alignment — and how replicating real brand UIs can supercharge your frontend skills.
